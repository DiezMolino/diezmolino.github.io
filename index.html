David Díez-del-Molino, PhD

Postdoctoral Researcher at the Swedish Museum of Natural History (NRM).


I am interested in the study of animal and human populations using a wide range of methods from population genetics to computational genomics. My current research is focused in two main fields: (1) studying the genetic and demographic history of invasive fish species aiming to discover the evolutionary processes that shape the genomes of invaders and drive their success in novel environments, and (2) using genomic tools to shed light on the origin and history of human and animal populations by investigating and modelling their genomes using ancient DNA.
