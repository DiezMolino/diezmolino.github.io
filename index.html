David Díez-del-Molino (• ◡•)/

PhD in Population Genetics. Postdoctoral Research Associate at UCL

I am interested in the study of animal and human populations through a wide range of mechanisms from population genetics to computational genomics. My research is focused on studying the genetic and demographic history of invasive fish species aiming to discover evolutionary processes that shape the genomes of invaders and drive its success in novel environments. Nowadays my research is moving towards using genomic tools to investigate the origin and history of human populations through looking up to their genomes and modelling ancientDNA.
