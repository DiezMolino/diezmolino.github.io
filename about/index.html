About me

I have a bachelor’s degree in Biological Sciences (University of Salamanca), a masters in Conservation Biology (University Complutense of Madrid) and a Ph.D. in Population Genetics (University of Girona). My main body of research has been focused on discovering the genetic basis of the evolutionary framework that underlies the successful life history of mosquitofish (Gambusia holbrooki), a highly aggressive invader in Mediterranean streams. I am currently doing research at the Molecular and Cultural Evolution Lab (University College London) leaded by Prof. Mark G. Thomas, as a Postdoctoral Research Associate. Under the framework of the Marie Curie - BEAN project (Bridging the European and Antolian Neolithic) I use computer simulations to model ancientDNA data retrieved from human remains to investigate past demographic events that shaped the history of Neolithic populations in Europe.
